Three female authors show us how writing can turn adversity into beauty

May 13, 2022 • In their latest works, Azar Nafisi, Elena Ferrante, and Anna Quindlen vigorously assert that reading and writing can pull us out of our mess. In their hands, reading and writing are worth celebrating.

Penguin Press

'Nasty, Brutish, and Short' contends that all parents are raising little philosophers

May 12, 2022 • Author Scott Hershovitz sets out to prove that philosophy, like inquisitive, rowdy children, can offer illuminating insights — even when addressing complex subjects related to misbehavior.

Broadside PR

In her new poems, Ada Limón argues for turning a delicate attention to the world

May 9, 2022 • The award-winning poet's new collection, The Hurting Kind, is a testament to the power of sensitivity and to the reality that the world is here to both guide us and lead us astray.

Berkley

Big city devotees find romance in a small town in Emily Henry's 'Book Lovers'

May 5, 2022 • The playful and clever story pokes holes in pop culture assumptions about small towns from Hallmark movies to romance novels as two rivals in the publishing world find love far from the city lights.

Metropolitan Books

Through art, 'Smahtguy' tells life story of former Rep. Barney Frank

May 4, 2022 • Eric Orner's book isn't just a great story, it's an enveloping visual experience crafted by a terrific artist; even if one paged through it without looking at the words, it would be a good read.

Delacorte Press

'Family Of Liars' reveals secrets at the core of the Sinclair family

May 3, 2022 • E. Lockhart's prequel to We Were Liars works perfectly well, too, as a standalone coming-of-age novel about grief, addiction, young love, and learning to navigate the world.

Penguin Press

'The Premonitions Bureau' considers whether some can sense future events

May 3, 2022 • Though winding at times, Sam Knight's book is thought-provoking and deeply researched, presenting the oddity of realized premonitions while allowing readers to come to their own conclusions.

Pantheon

'Companion Piece' connects two plagues and two female artists five centuries apart

May 2, 2022 • By exploring binaries such as imagination versus reality and surface versus depth — with their often blurred boundaries — Ali Smith's latest challenges readers to embrace the indeterminate.

Berkley

'The No-Show' is an adventure in romantic storytelling

May 1, 2022 • At its best, Beth O'Leary's tender and fragmented narrative feels like a metaphor for experience — how we only ever know part of the story of our lives and control even less.

Tor Books

'Nettle & Bone' creates a once-upon-a-time that is familiar, yet original

April 27, 2022 • T. Kingfisher treats source material like a buffet; the result feels like a cozy but still perilous D&D adventure, full of found-family, second chances, and winks to the folklore that inspired it.

Simon & Schuster

In 'Tasha,' a son tries to make sense of his smart, difficult mother

Fresh Air

April 26, 2022 • In this droll, emotionally wrenching and profound memoir, novelist Brian Morton attempts to see his mother as a whole person — not just in relation to him, or, God forbid, as an eccentric "character."

In 'Tasha,' a son tries to make sense of his smart, difficult mother

This Earth Day, one book presents global warming and climate justice as inseparable

There's no redemption to be had in Chelsea Bieker's 'Heartbroke'

April 7, 2022 • The author of Godshot brings readers a new set of stories; Heartbroke unfolds in a chorus of yearning and sorrow, told in 11 different voices that Chelsea Bieker inhabits with perfect pitch.

Scribner

'The Candy House' is a brilliant portrait of intersecting lives

April 6, 2022 • In Jennifer Egan's novel, there is a persistent, lovely countermelody to the corporate project of mapping human experience; it's full of people engaged in a sweeter and more plaintive human algebra.

Penguin Random House

In 'Time is a Mother,' poet Ocean Vuong reflects on life, and time, without his mom

April 5, 2022 • In his new collection, poet Ocean Vuong transforms Time, normally associated with death and erasure, into a mother with endlessly regenerative power.

Knopf

'Sea of Tranquility' reflects our pandemic woes through a time-travel lens

April 4, 2022 • Station Eleven author Emily St. John Mandel wrote a book during these last two years of social isolation — about big moments in our lives and small moments in time.

Random House

In 'Ancestor Trouble,' Maud Newton wrestles with her family history

March 29, 2022 • Maud Newton spent decades researching genealogic records, genetic science, and the cultural history of "ancestor hunger"; her book is also a coming-to-terms with how to face and honor family history.
